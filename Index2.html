“Maverick Steele Rescues Flooded Montana Town—Leads Operation Personally”

March 2024 – Kalispell, MT

In a moment that showcased both his leadership and unshakable grit, presidential candidate Maverick Steele was on the front lines this week after flash flooding devastated parts of Flathead County.

Steele—best known nationally for his tough stance on border security and economic reform—is also a longtime Montanan and former Green Beret. And this week, he proved once again that he doesn't just talk about leadership—he lives it.

“He Didn’t Wait for Permission”

As local officials scrambled to assess damage, Steele mobilized a volunteer relief crew using trucks, generators, and supplies from his own ranch supply business. Wearing work gloves and combat boots, he was photographed wading through knee-deep water to help evacuate elderly residents from a mobile home park.

“He didn’t wait for permission,” said Sheriff Ray Dobson. “He just loaded up and started getting people out. We had first responders delayed by the washed-out bridge—and Maverick was already there.”

Bringing the Power Back

With power lines down across large parts of the area, Steele coordinated with linemen and personally delivered backup generators to two rural clinics and the Columbia Falls Fire Department.

“His team had chainsaws, pumps, and fuel faster than FEMA,” said Fire Captain Lisa Herrera. “And he made sure they had coffee and hot meals, too.”

A Different Kind of Candidate

In an election cycle where photo ops and soundbites dominate headlines, Steele’s actions have caught national attention. A video of him pulling a soaked golden retriever from a flooded trailer has gone viral, drawing praise across party lines.

“This is the kind of man we need in office,” said Montana native and veteran Alan White. “He’s not afraid to lead from the front. He’s not a politician—he’s a patriot.”

Final Word

When asked by a reporter why he jumped into action, Steele simply replied:

“When your neighbors are in trouble, you don’t wait on Washington—you go help. That’s what real Americans do.”
